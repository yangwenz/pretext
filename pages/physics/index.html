Physics Text Demos

Interactive demos of the @chenglou/pretext/physics engine. Characters are rigid bodies with real forces, collisions, and constraints.

G

Gravity Drop

A paragraph of text where every letter is a physics body. Click to release them — they fall, bounce off walls, and pile up on the floor.

gravity collision bounds
S

Shatter

Click anywhere on the text to explode letters outward with a radial impulse. Click again to reassemble them back into readable text.

impulse springs reassemble
M

Magnetic Cursor

Move your mouse over the text. Letters are repelled by the cursor and spring back to their rest positions when you move away.

repulsor springs interaction
B

Rope Bridge

Words strung as rope bridges between fixed anchor points. Gravity pulls them into a natural catenary curve. Drag letters to disturb them.

rigid chains gravity drag
W

Wind

Text buffeted by gusting wind. Characters sway and rotate, tethered by springs that pull them back toward their reading positions.

wind springs damping
A

Word Arena

Eight words form rigid objects that tumble and collide. Each word's characters are locked together; different words bounce off each other.

rigid bodies collision groups impulse
S

Character Shapes

Characters arranged as circles, boxes, and triangles with rigid cross-bracing. Drop shapes and watch them stack, tumble, and collide.

geometry rigid constraints stacking
N

Newton's Cradle

Five character balls on rope pendulums. Pull one aside and release to watch momentum transfer through the chain on collision.

rope momentum collision
O

Orbital Text

Words orbit a central star under gravitational attraction. Click to add gravitational pull. Watch orbital mechanics play out with text.

gravity orbits n-body
R

Live Reflow

Pretext lays out a paragraph, then physics shatters it. Resize the container width and watch characters fly back into the new layout positions.

pretext layout spring assembly
D

Document Cradle

A Newton's Cradle embedded inline within a pretext-rendered document. Text flows around the interactive physics widget.

pretext pendulum inline widget
